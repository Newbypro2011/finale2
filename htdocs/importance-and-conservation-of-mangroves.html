Importance of Mangroves and Conservation Efforts

Why Mangroves Matter

Mangroves were once generally dismissed as "swampy wastelands" due to its appearance, not because of its function. Mangrove forests are important for the following reasons:

  • Protects coastlines by reducing erosion and buffering storm surges and flooding.
  • Filters pollutants and trap sediments, improving local water quality.
  • Serves as nurseries and habitats, supporting diverse marine and bird species.
  • Acts as highly efficient carbon sinks, storing more carbon than many other forest types and helping fight climate change

Conservation Efforts in Davao City

  • In Barangay Lasang, community efforts have been implemented by doubling the mangrove forest size. This effort began in 2013 through strong local participation and enforcement of environmental laws.
  • IDIS, an environmental group that advocated for urban wetland protection within the city. This resulted in a 2019-2028 zoning ordinance that prohibits reclaimation in the city's mangrove forests.
  • Volunteer groups such as ASEZ WAO have organized large-scale mangrove activities. This includes planting over 1,000 mangrove seedlings at the Bucana Coastal Area.
  • Last but not the least, the City Environmental and Natural Resources Office (CENRO) routinely plants thousands of mangrove seedlings in the coastal areas to maintain the city's mangroves.
